What Types of User Information Does Salesflare Obtain?

Visitors to the Salesflare Website. If you are a visitor to the Salesflare website or a Salesflare client (Client) who registers or signs into the Salesflare website (including for purposes of obtaining a free trial of Salesflares services), we may obtain the following types of information from you or concerning your computer or device (Salesflare Client Information), which may include information that can itself be used to identify and/or contact you (Personally Identifiable Information):

         Name

         Email address

         Phone number

         Website name and URL

         Credit card number

         Billing address

         Visitor site preferences

 

Salesflare may also receive certain information that may be provided by your browser or mobile device, including:

         Browser information

         Operating system information

         Mobile device information (e.g., device identifier, mobile operating system, etc.)

         IP address

         Pages accessed

         Time of visit

         Time of last visit

         Referring site, application, or service, including the relevant search queries that led you to Salesflares website

 

Visitors to Salesflares Clients Websites. Our Clients may use customized and proprietary Salesflares code, software and/or services (Service(s)) to obtain information regarding the activities that users engage in while visiting their web pages (Client User Data). When a user visits a website that uses Salesflares Service, Salesflare code contained on the website contacts Salesflares servers and enables Salesflare to collect Client User Data. Some Clients may also use their own code to obtain Client User Data and then send such information to Salesflares servers. Client User Data may include Personally Identifiable Information, including, but not limited to, the types of information listed for Salesflare Client Information above.

Client User Data is stored on Salesflares web servers and databases (including those hosted on third party servers on behalf of Salesflare) for use in performing analysis and producing reports for the applicable Salesflare Client. In addition, Salesflare itself uses the Salesflare Service in order to obtain insights into visitors activities on its own websites, which include, as of the Effective Date, http://salesflare.com and http://docflare.com.

Salesflares collection and analysis of Client User Data helps our Clients to evaluate and understand how their users are interacting with their websites, and potentially, to modify their websites in order to make them more valuable or useful for their users. Salesflares Clients determine the types of Client User Data that are sent to Salesfares servers for analysis. This data may include or be linked to Personally Identifiable Information depending on how the Client uses Salesflares Services on its website(s). Salesflares Clients are contractually prohibited from sending personally sensitive Client User Data (e.g. personal health information, political opinions, religious or philosophical beliefs) to Salesflare and Salesflare does not knowingly receive such data.

In addition, Salesflare may collect certain information about a Clients visitors computer that may be provided by the visitors browser. This includes browser information, operating system information, mobile device information, IP address, time of visit, and the referring site, application, or service. Such information may be collected and shared with the Salesflare Client even though not specifically requested by the Client.

Salesflares Use of Cookies, Local Storage Files and Web Beacons

Cookies and local storage files. Salesflare uses cookies and local storage files (small text files that Salesflare stores locally on your computer) on our Clients websites for one or more of the following purposes: to help identify unique visitors and/or devices; assess usage patterns and perform traffic analysis; identify visitor preferences; conduct A/B testing; diagnose problems with our servers, and otherwise administer the Salesflare Service. Salesflare does not use methods such as browser cache, Flash cookies, or eTags, for acquiring or storing information about end users web browsing activity on Client websites or otherwise.

Depending on how our Clients use Salesflares Services, information contained in Salesflare cookies and/or local storage files placed on the computers of end users may be linked to Personally Identifiable Information in Salesflares database. This allows our Clients to use the Salesflare Service to better analyze and measure their users interactions with their website and to identify activities by the same user on their website across time.

If you do not wish to have Salesflare place and use cookies and/or local storage files on your computer, you should set your browser preferences to refuse all cookies and/or local storage files before accessing Salesflare or other websites that may use Salesflare. Salesflare is not responsible for any failure by you or your browser to accurately implement or communicate your browser preferences or settings. In addition, please be aware that even if you configure your browser settings to reject all cookies and/or local storage files, your activity on Salesflare client websites will still be recorded by our Service. However, your rejection of all cookies and/or local storage files will prevent Salesflare from determining whether or not someone using your computer has previously visited the website, i.e., whether or not the visitor is a unique visitor, and from retrieving any information about your prior activities on a Clients website through information contained in a cookie and/or local storage files.

Please note that if you set your browser to reject cookies and/or local storage files, some features of the websites you visit may be unavailable. For more information on how to reject cookies and/or local storage files, see your browser's instructions on changing your cookie and/or local storage file settings.

If you do not elect to set your browser preferences to refuse cookies and/or local storage files, you consent to have cookies and/or local storage files, including cookies and/or local storage files used by Salesflare, placed on your computer when you visit our Clients websites.

Web Beacons. Salesflare may also collect certain information using Web Beacons. Web Beacons are electronic images that may be used on Salesflares Website, in Salesflares Service, or in our email communications. They are used, for example, to count website visits or to tell if an email has been opened and acted upon.

User Privacy Controls and Do Not Track Signals

Clients and Visitors to the Salesflare Website. Salesflare offers several ways for you to review and update account information that is obtained and stored by Salesflare, or to change your Salesflare communication preferences:

         If you have a Salesflare account, you can review or update your account information by contacting us at info@salesflare.com.

         You may unsubscribe to any of our e-mail or direct message communications regarding updates or products by following the unsubscribe instructions in the body of any message.

         You may contact us at info@salesflare.com, attn: Salesflare Privacy Office.

Do Not Track Signals. Salesflare Clients decide whether their use of Salesflares Service is responsive to Do Not Track signals from browsers, and whether Salesflare will be notified of a Do Not Track signal received from a users browser. If a Salesflare Client enables the forwarding of a Do Not Track signal to Salesflare, Salesflare will attempt to read the signal and will not collect any Client User Data from a user visiting that Salesflare Clients website during that session, following receipt of the Do Not Track signal. The Do Not Track signal is browser-specific, and will not work for mobile devices. If a Salesflare Client does not forward Do Not Track signals to Salesflare, then Salesflare will not have any access to such signals from the Clients users, and such signal will have no effect on Saleslflares collection or use of data received from that user during the applicable session(s).
